WORKING AT A MOVIE THEATER

Movie theaters are usually a summer time job for high school students and college students. Knowing this, most people do not really care about the job.It does not help that employees are the scapegoats for guests to express their anger from whereever it may come from. Maybe they are having a bad day. Or maybe the movie was horrible. The most commont complaint is the prices. It is interesting how guests express their concern about the prices to the employees when the employees have no power. And what is more interesting is that knowing these prices are high, people still buy these high priced items.

As one of the college students working at a movie theater staffed by mostly high schoolers, I made it my goal to be a role model and show the correct way of going to work. It does not matter what the pay is. What matters is putting out your best performance to correctly represent yourself and the company and to make the people you are serving happy. Not many people have this mindset. However, with the respect I have garnished, I believe I have shown these high schoolers what hard work, punctuality, and a positive attitude can do.

With the inventions of smartphones, movies can be streamed anywhere. Therefore, movie theaters have to improve their movies experiences with state of the art technology. Having worked at a theater with a brand new IMAX and Dolby room, I can say that the classic American movie theater has come a long way. Technology has the power to improve the common day experience and elevate it to something completely new. What comes next for the movie industry is fascinating. Will there be other senses like smell added to the experience? Will the seats move along to the movie to the point it is a ride?
